\section{Conditional low-acceleration matter coupling}

\subsection{Inverse construction of the cubic-gradient operator}

Let $q=|\nabla\psi|$ and consider the static phonon action
\begin{equation}
 S_\psi=\int \dd t\,\dd^3x\left[-K(q)-C_m\rho_b\psi\right].
 \label{eq:static-general}
\end{equation}
Variation yields
\begin{equation}
 \nabla\cdot\left[\frac{K'(q)}{q}\nabla\psi\right]=C_m\rho_b.
 \label{eq:general-field}
\end{equation}
For a spherical local source, requiring
\begin{equation}
 C_m q=C_m\sqrt{a_0g_N}
\end{equation}
fixes, in the convention $C_m=C_{\rm IR}$,
\begin{equation}
 K(q)=\frac{C_{\rm IR}}{12\pi Ga_0}q^3.
 \label{eq:cubic-K}
\end{equation}

The complete static weak-field action in this normalization is
\begin{align}
 S_{\rm WF}=\int \dd t\,\dd^3x\bigg[
 &-\frac{|\nabla\Phi_N|^2}{8\pi G}-\rho_b\Phi_N \nonumber\\
 &-\frac{C_{\rm IR}}{12\pi Ga_0}|\nabla\psi|^3
 -C_m\rho_b\psi\bigg].
 \label{eq:wf-action}
\end{align}
When $C_m=C_{\rm IR}$, the sourced equation becomes
\begin{equation}
 \nabla\cdot\left[\frac{|\nabla\psi|}{a_0}\nabla\psi\right]
 =4\pi G\rho_b.
 \label{eq:p3-local}
\end{equation}
For spherical symmetry this gives
\begin{equation}
 |\nabla\psi|^2=a_0g_N,
 \qquad
 g_P=C_m\sqrt{a_0g_N}.
 \label{eq:square-root}
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:square-root} is an action-level result for the conditional
IR theory, not a derivation of $C_m=2/3$.

\subsection{Compact toroidal source}

On compact $\Tthree$ there is no boundary at spatial infinity, and a periodic
divergence integrates to zero.  Write
\begin{equation}
 \rho_b=\bar\rho_b+\delta\rho_b,
 \qquad
 \psi=\bar\psi(t)+\varphi(t,\mathbf x),
\end{equation}
with zero spatial averages for $\delta\rho_b$ and $\varphi$.  The local
periodic equation has the form
\begin{equation}
 D_i\left[\frac{|D\varphi|}{a_0}D^i\varphi\right]
 =4\pi G\delta\rho_b+\mathcal S_Q,
 \qquad
 \int_{\Tthree}(4\pi G\delta\rho_b+\mathcal S_Q)\,\dd V=0.
 \label{eq:periodic-p3}
\end{equation}
The homogeneous mode belongs to the cosmological/open-system problem.  A
localized spherical solution is therefore the small-region,
compensated-overdensity limit of Eq.~\eqref{eq:periodic-p3}, not an isolated
global cosmology.

\subsection{Disk geometry and coefficient status}

For a general density distribution, equality of divergences permits
\begin{equation}
 \frac{|\nabla\varphi|}{a_0}\nabla\varphi
 =\nabla\Phi_N+\nabla\times\mathbf H.
 \label{eq:curl-field}
\end{equation}
The solenoidal field vanishes in sufficient symmetry but is generally nonzero
for a finite disk.  Consequently a pointwise square-root prescription is a
historical algebraic approximation.  DISK-001 must solve
Eq.~\eqref{eq:periodic-p3} for realistic baryonic sources before $C_m=2/3$ and
$C_m\simeq1$ can be fairly compared.

The coefficient also changes under a rescaling of $\psi$ unless the kinetic
and matter vertices are fixed together.  The trace ratio $2/3$ remains a
geometric motivation and a matching hypothesis, not a completed local Wilson
coefficient.
