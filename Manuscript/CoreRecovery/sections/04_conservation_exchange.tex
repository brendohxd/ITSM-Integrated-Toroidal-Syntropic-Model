\section{Conservation and syntropic throughput}

The observable matter--plenum subsystem may exchange energy and momentum with
an external reservoir sector, but the complete system must satisfy the
contracted Bianchi identity.  We therefore distinguish two currents:
\begin{align}
 \nabla_\mu T_m^{\mu\nu} &= Q_{\rm mp}^{\nu},
 \label{eq:qmp}\\
 \nabla_\mu T_P^{\mu\nu} &= -Q_{\rm mp}^{\nu}+Q_{\rm syn}^{\nu},
 \label{eq:qp}\\
 \nabla_\mu T_R^{\mu\nu} &= -Q_{\rm syn}^{\nu}.
 \label{eq:qr}
\end{align}
Adding Eqs.~\eqref{eq:qmp}--\eqref{eq:qr} gives
\begin{equation}
 \nabla_\mu\left(T_m^{\mu\nu}+T_P^{\mu\nu}+T_R^{\mu\nu}\right)=0.
 \label{eq:total-conservation}
\end{equation}

$Q_{\rm mp}^{\nu}$ is the local matter--plenum exchange generated by the same
interaction that sources the phonon.  $Q_{\rm syn}^{\nu}$ is reservoir
throughput.  Treating them as one undifferentiated $Q^\nu$ hides two different
physical jobs and prevents a clean local/cosmological split.

\subsection{Candidate local interaction}

A universal conformal matter metric illustrates how a force and an exchange
current can share an origin,
\begin{equation}
 \widetilde g_{\mu\nu}=e^{2C_m\psi/c^2}g_{\mu\nu},
 \qquad
 S_m=S_m[\widetilde g_{\mu\nu},\Psi_m].
 \label{eq:conformal-candidate}
\end{equation}
For pressureless matter its nonrelativistic limit contains
\begin{equation}
 \mathcal L_{\rm int}^{\rm NR}=-C_m\rho_b\psi,
\end{equation}
and its exchange current is proportional to the matter trace times
$\nabla^\nu\psi$.  This is a candidate matter coupling, not a complete
relativistic phenomenology: a purely conformal rescaling does not by itself
provide the required lensing.

\subsection{Reservoir throughput and anisotropic stress}

In the plenum rest frame a general stress tensor includes
\begin{equation}
 T_P^{\mu\nu}=\rho_P U^\mu U^\nu+p_Ph^{\mu\nu}+\pi^{\mu\nu}.
 \label{eq:plenum-stress}
\end{equation}
The traceless spatial tensor $\pi^{\mu\nu}$, not the energy-transfer vector
alone, carries directional stress.  In a biaxial state one may define
\begin{equation}
 \Pi=p_t-p_p,
 \qquad
 \pi^i{}_j=\mathrm{diag}\left(-\frac{2\Pi}{3},\frac{\Pi}{3},\frac{\Pi}{3}\right).
 \label{eq:biaxial-pi}
\end{equation}
A relation such as $Q_{\rm syn}^{\nu}\propto\Theta U^\nu$ injects energy along
the background flow but does not determine $\Pi$.  A geometry-dependent
action or constitutive law is required to convert throughput into shear.

No fixed redshift law for $Q_{\rm syn}^{\nu}$ is adopted in this alpha.  Its
background and perturbation dependence must follow from the reservoir model,
and local decoupling must be tested rather than asserted from $\Theta\to0$.
